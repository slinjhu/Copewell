Definitions

  • Mean: $ \mu = E[X] $
  • STD (Standard deviation): $\sigma = \sqrt{E[(X-\mu)^2]}$
  • Skewness (third standardized moment): $\gamma_1 = E\left[ \left( {X-\mu\over\sigma} \right)^3 \right]$
  • CV (Coefficient of Variation, aka relative standard deviation): $\sigma / \mu$

Covariance, correlation and Cronbach's alpha

  • Covariance: $\mbox{cov}(X, Y) = E[(X - E[X]) \cdot (Y - E[Y]) ]$
  • Pearson's correlation coefficient: $\rho_{X,Y} = \mbox{cov}(X,Y) / (\sigma_X \sigma_Y)$
  • Cronbach's alpha: a measure of internal consistency. Suppose we have $X = Y_1 + Y_2 +\dots + Y_K$, then: \[ \alpha = {K\over K-1} \left( 1 - {\sum_{i-1}^K \sigma^2_{Y_i} \over \sigma^2_X } \right) \] Usually $\alpha \ge 0.7$ means acceptable and $\alpha \le 0.5$ means not acceptable.

Aggregation process

  1. Treat negatives and zeros as missing value
  2. Box-Cox transform each measures
  3. Truncate with $\pm3.5$ std
  4. Adjust for direction
  5. Calculate mean of available measures in each subdomain
  6. Calculate mean of all available subdomains in each domain
